How Europeans Came to India

The Age of European Colonialism (1498-1947)

Timeline Overview

  • 1498: Portuguese arrive (Vasco da Gama)
  • 1605: Dutch establish first factory
  • 1664: French East India Company formed
  • 1600: British East India Company founded
  • 1757: Battle of Plassey - British dominance begins
  • 1947: Indian Independence

Why Did Europe Want India?

  • Spice Trade - pepper, cinnamon, cardamom
  • Textiles and luxury goods
  • Bypass Arab middlemen
  • Massive profits (up to 90% for spices)
  • Strategic location for Asian trade

The Age of Exploration

  • 15th century: Europeans seek sea routes to Asia
  • Fall of Constantinople (1453) disrupted land routes
  • Portugal leads maritime exploration
  • Competition for trade dominance
  • Advanced naval technology gave Europeans advantage

Portuguese Arrival (1498)

  • Vasco da Gama lands at Calicut (Kozhikode) - May 20, 1498
  • First European to reach India by sea
  • Met by Zamorin (local ruler)
  • Opened direct maritime trade route
  • End of Arab-Venetian trade monopoly

Portuguese Dominance (1500-1600)

  • 1510: Captured Goa from Bijapur Sultanate
  • Built forts along Malabar coast
  • Established Estado da Índia
  • St. Francis Xavier brought Christianity (1542)
  • Controlled key ports: Goa, Diu, Daman

Dutch Arrival (1605)

  • Dutch East India Company (VOC) formed 1602
  • First factory at Masulipatnam (1605)
  • World's first multinational corporation
  • Focused on spice trade monopoly
  • Gradually lost to British by 1825

French Entry (1664)

  • French East India Company established 1664
  • François Martin founded Pondicherry (1674)
  • Chief settlement in India
  • Also controlled Mahe, Yanam, Karaikal
  • Lost to British in Anglo-French wars

British East India Company (1600)

  • Founded December 31, 1600 by royal charter
  • First trading post at Surat (1608)
  • Three presidency towns: Calcutta, Bombay, Madras
  • From trading company to ruling power
  • Lasted until 1858

Competition for Control

  • Multiple European powers competed
  • Anglo-French rivalry most intense
  • Each built forts and armies
  • Local rulers played Europeans against each other
  • Winner would control vast wealth

Battle of Plassey (1757) - The Turning Point

  • Robert Clive vs. Nawab Siraj-ud-Daulah
  • Clive's 3,000 troops vs. 50,000 enemy forces
  • Victory through conspiracy and betrayal
  • Mir Jafar became puppet Nawab
  • Britain gained control of wealthy Bengal

How Britain Won

  • Superior naval power
  • Better military organization
  • Political alliances and bribery
  • Economic resources from Bengal
  • Defeated French in Seven Years War
  • Subsidiary Alliance System

Indian Resistance

  • Tipu Sultan - 'Tiger of Mysore'
  • Used modern weapons including rockets
  • Sought alliances with France and Ottoman Empire
  • Four Anglo-Mysore Wars (1767-1799)
  • Died fighting at Seringapatam (1799)

Colonial Administration

  • Company Rule (1757-1858)
  • Governor-Generals appointed
  • Civil and military services
  • Revenue collection systems
  • English law and education
  • Infrastructure development

Economic Impact

  • Massive wealth extraction to Europe
  • De-industrialization of India
  • Agriculture focused on cash crops
  • Monopoly over trade
  • Decline of traditional industries
  • Famines due to policy failures

Cultural Exchange

  • European languages (English, Portuguese, French)
  • Christianity spread
  • Western education system
  • Architecture - Indo-European styles
  • Legal systems
  • Scientific knowledge exchange

Timeline Summary

  • 1498-1600: Portuguese dominance
  • 1600-1750: Multiple European powers compete
  • 1757-1858: British East India Company rule
  • 1858-1947: British Crown rule (British Raj)
  • 1947: Independence and Partition

Path to Independence

  • Growing Indian nationalism
  • Freedom movements and leaders
  • World Wars weakened European powers
  • Non-violent resistance movements
  • 1947: India and Pakistan independence
  • End of European colonial era

Key Lessons

  • Economic motivations drove colonialism
  • Technological advantages helped Europeans
  • Disunity among local rulers was exploited
  • Resistance movements eventually succeeded
  • Colonial legacy still influences modern India

Discussion Questions

  • How might Indian history be different if Europeans never arrived?
  • What were the positive and negative impacts of colonialism?
  • Why were Europeans successful despite being outnumbered?
  • How did Indian resistance evolve over time?
  • What lessons apply to the modern world?
